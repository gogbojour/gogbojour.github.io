Taken together, these aren't scattered complaints — they cluster around three points: getting in (signup/OTP/OAuth), getting a ride (driver liquidity), and being heard when something breaks (the canned review replies). Fixing those three in order is the fastest path to a materially better rating.

02

App fixes — proposed scope

Sign-up & identity

  • Add Google Sign-In (OAuth 2.0), positioned as the primary path with OTP as fallback — removes the single most-requested missing feature.
  • Rebuild the OTP screen so every control, including "Resend OTP," sits above the device's safe-area/navigation bar on all screen sizes.
  • Add visible delivery status (sending / sent / failed) so users aren't left guessing whether an OTP is coming.

Verification & payments

  • Full device-matrix QA on the verification flow, replacing silent hangs with clear timeout and retry states.
  • Trace and repair the student discount signup so the reward actually reflects at checkout.
  • Audit the add-card flow end to end and harden error handling around failed transactions.

Driver liquidity

  • Review matching radius, queueing, and driver incentive structure so a "no drivers found" moment offers a next step — wait, widen search, or notify me — instead of a dead end.

Community trust

  • Replace the generic "please update and rate 5 stars" reply pattern with responses tied to actual release notes, once a fix ships.
03

A website Bro. doesn't have yet

Right now, anyone who hears about Bro. and searches for it online has nowhere authoritative to land. A proper website closes that gap and does real commercial work:

  • A landing page with a clear download link, service area, and fare explanation — the first-impression asset for press, partners, and word-of-mouth traffic.
  • A driver sign-up funnel, separate from the passenger story, aimed at solving the liquidity problem from the supply side.
  • An FAQ and support page that answers the exact questions showing up in reviews today — reducing repeat support tickets before they're even opened.
  • Built on the same lean stack used across all of Dalton Digital's work — fast to ship and easy to hand over.
04

A virtual call centre — IVR and virtual PABX

Bro. currently has no dedicated line for riders or drivers to call when something goes wrong — today, that gap is filled by app-store reviews, which is the worst possible channel for it. The proposal:

  • A virtual PABX (cloud phone system) giving Bro. a single support number, with extensions for rider support, driver support, and escalations — no physical hardware or office required.
  • An IVR (interactive voice response) menu routing callers automatically: report a trip issue, driver support, account/OTP help, or agent.
  • Call logging and missed-call callback queueing, so no rider or driver complaint disappears silently — the same failure mode as the current review-reply pattern, solved at the source.
  • Scales with the business: the same system supports one agent today or a small team later, without re-platforming.
